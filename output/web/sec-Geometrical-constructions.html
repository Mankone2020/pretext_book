    Subsection 4.2.7 Constructing triangles

    Definition 4.2.2.

    A triangle is a basic shape with three sides and three angles. The three sides of a triangle can have different lengths, and the angles can vary in size, but the sum of the angles in any triangle is always \(180^\circ\text{.}\)
    Types of Triangles
    1. Equilateral Triangle : All three sides and angles are equal.
    2. Isosceles Triangle : Two sides are equal in length, and two angles are equal.
    3. Right Triangle : One angle is exactly \(90^\circ\text{.}\)
    4. Acute Triangle : All angles are less than \(90^\circ\text{.}\)
    5. Scalene Triangle : All three sides and angles are different.
    6. Obtuse Triangle : One angle is greater than \(90^\circ\text{.}\)
